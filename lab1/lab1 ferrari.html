Ferrari is an iconic Italian sports car manufacturer known for producing high-performance luxury vehicles. Here's some key information about Ferrari:

History: Ferrari was founded in 1939 by Enzo Ferrari as Auto Avio Costruzioni Ferrari, primarily to produce racing cars. The first Ferrari car, the 125 S, was built in 1947.

Headquarters: Ferrari is headquartered in Maranello, Italy, and has a strong presence in the world of Formula One racing.

Logo: The famous Ferrari logo features a prancing horse (Cavallino Rampante) on a yellow background. This logo was adopted in 1947 and is inspired by the emblem of an Italian World War I flying ace, Count Francesco Baracca.
