Summary of potential benefits of avoiding flexbox or grid layout in HTML:

Simplicity for Simple Layouts: For straightforward layouts, avoiding flexbox or grid can lead to simpler and lighter code, reducing complexity and development time.
Legacy Browser Support: If you need to support very old browsers that lack full flexbox or grid support, avoiding these technologies may be necessary to ensure compatibility.
Familiarity: Developers who are more comfortable with traditional CSS layout methods may find it easier to work with established techniques like floats, positioning, and inline-blocks.
Reduced Learning Curve: Beginners in web development may find traditional layout methods more accessible and less daunting compared to learning the intricacies of flexbox or grid.
Performance Optimization: In some cases, for extremely performance-critical applications, avoiding complex layout technologies can lead to faster page rendering.
Specific Use Cases: Certain design requirements or constraints may necessitate a non-flexbox or non-grid approach, depending on the project's unique needs.
